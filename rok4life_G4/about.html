About

  • Rock for saving lives - our story A person who has not yet joined a bone marrow pool will never know if he can easily save the life of a patient who is genetically similar to him. Salvage for Life was established in order to raise awareness in Israel of the transformation that has taken place in recent years in bone marrow donation and the simple process of joining the Hadassah Eli Elkoubi bone marrow pool, which is carried out through a simple saliva test. Rock for Life Saving wishes to add any male or female citizen in Israel in good health, aged 18-45 who have not yet joined one of the reserves in Israel. A simple saliva test can save lives! What is bone marrow donation? Bone marrow consists of spongy material, is found in the drum of the bones and is the place of formation of red cells, white cells and blood platelets. Blood cells that are in the earliest stage of development in the bone marrow and produce all blood cells are called progenitor cells. In recent years, there has been enormous progress in the healing of serious diseases related to the blood through the transplantation of stem cells from a healthy person into the body of a sick person, when it is essential that there be a match between them in the classification of the tissues. Only a small part of all patients who need a stem cell transplant have a family member who matches their tissue classification, and for all the others it is necessary to look for a donor in the general population who is identical to them in tissue classification, when he usually belongs to the same ethnic group to which the patient belongs. Hence the need and importance of having a pool that will include a large number of volunteers (between the ages of 18 and 45) with a known tissue classification who are willing to donate stem cells (bone marrow cells) if they are found to be compatible with the patient in need of this donation. Donating stem cells is a routine and accepted process in Israel and the wider world, which is carried out through a vein similar to donating blood, where tens of thousands of collections have been carried out to date. The stem cells regenerate and supplement the donated amount in a short time and the donor gets back everything he donated. How do you join the pool of donors? Providing a saliva - oral mucosa sample (DNA) to perform a tissue classification test. The results of the tissue classification test will be transferred to a computerized donor database that serves all patients in need of stem cell donation in Israel and around the world. How do you locate a donor from the database? When a patient needs a donation, the database is scanned to find a donor whose tissue classification matches the patient's. If such a donor is found in the database, he is asked to donate a normal blood sample, with which final compatibility tests will be performed. If these tests show compatibility with the patient's tissue classification, the process will continue towards collecting cells. How is the stem cell collection process carried out? After a suitable donor is found, the following actions will be performed: For five days before the collection, the donor will be injected with a substance that encourages cell growth. On the fifth day, the cells are collected through the arm vein - similar to a normal blood donation - in a process that lasts several hours. (For the donor's choice, there is an alternative way to make the donation. This is done by pumping cells from the pelvic cavity under regional anesthesia of the lower body - this process requires one day of hospitalization)
